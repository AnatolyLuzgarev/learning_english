  • Reading

    1. Vocabulary question

      Examples: (we should define the meaning of the word or the phrase)
      1. Word ..... has the closest meaning to word ..........
      2. In stating ....... the author means that ........
      Look at the sentence. If meaning is vague, look at the neighbour sentences. Don't choose word you don't know, if another variant looks appropriate.

    2. Detail question

      Look at the question and find key words.
      Look paragraph for key words or their synonyms.
      (Usually 1 sentence, may be 2)

    3. Negative detail question

      Example:
      All of the following are mentioned in the passage ......, EXCEPT .......
      Strategy:
      Look at the question and find its key words. If you see clear key words, then:
      Begin to read the paragraph, looking for key words or synonyms. If find key words, read the sentence and understand it, further look at the answer choice and eliminate it, if it corresponds to the information in the sentence.
      If key words can't help significally, find key word for every answer choice and look them in the text.

    4. Inference question

      The information you need There are two kinds of questions: inference form fact and from paragraph entirely. Entire paragraph: read the paragraph, understand the meaning. If answer is not obvious, eliminate wrong answers. From farc: find key words in the question, find them in the paragraph and understand what is implied from that. It is not stated in the text directly. Such variant will be incorrect.

    5. Reference question

      If word is noun, determine whether it is a single or plural.

    6. Plug-in questions

      There are three kinds of sentences:
      1. Topic sentence. Sentence which is supposed to be at the beginning, must describe a topic in general
      2. Supporting detail
      3. Conclusion. It may be or detail of final idea, or conclusion (summarizing)
        4. At first, read the sentence attentively and find there references and transitions or the. Begin read sentences, looking for logical chains.

    8. Function questions

      Examples:
      1. Author mention ......... in order to ..............

    9. Summary questions

      We should find the details among the answers.
      • If the answer choice doesn't pertain to the summary, we should eliminate it
      • It detail is not abstract, we should eliminate it, for example:
        The low temperature causes the dilatation of the water.
      • If detail is abstract, for example: This effect was caused by harsh conditions, low morale and low salary, we can't eliminate it at once.
      • Examples, but not facts from the paragraph, names and so forth
